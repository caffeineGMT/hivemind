## Description (3 paragraphs, ~300 words)

### What It Is
Hivemind Engine is an autonomous AI orchestrator that builds and runs entire businesses for you. Instead of manually configuring prompts or chaining tools, you describe your business goal ("Launch an AI newsletter" or "Build a customer support bot"), and Hivemind spawns a team of specialized Claude agents that work together to make it happen—writing code, scraping data, generating content, sending emails, and deploying to production. Every agent runs in its own tmux session with isolated state, coordinated through a real-time WebSocket dashboard where you can monitor tasks, costs, and performance.

### Who It's For
If you're a developer, entrepreneur, or AI tinkerer who wants to launch AI-powered side projects without the overhead of managing infrastructure, prompt engineering, or agent coordination, Hivemind is for you. It's perfect for:
- **Solo founders** launching AI SaaS products (newsletters, content tools, scrapers)
- **Developers** who want to ship AI features without building orchestration from scratch
- **Agencies** managing multiple AI-driven client projects
- **Researchers** running autonomous experiments with multi-agent systems

### Why It's Different
Unlike Auto-GPT (requires manual config), LangChain (just a framework), or ChatGPT (single-agent), Hivemind is a **complete end-to-end platform**. It handles agent spawning, inter-agent communication, error recovery, cost tracking, deployment, and monitoring—all out of the box. Agents self-heal when tasks fail, automatically retry with exponential backoff, and share context via a message bus. The open-source core (MIT license) means you can self-host everything, while the hosted dashboard provides a Vercel-like DX for AI orchestration. We've had beta users launch AI newsletters, Instagram bots, and research assistants in under 30 minutes.
